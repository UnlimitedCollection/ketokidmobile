KetoKid Care: Doctor Web Development Plan
This plan outlines the core features and data architecture for the Doctor-Side Webview of the KetoKid Care application. The design follows the "Empathetic Guardian" philosophy, balancing clinical precision with a supportive, high-end wellness aesthetic.

Core Features
1. Unified Patient Dashboard
A high-level view for clinicians to monitor their entire roster of pediatric patients at a glance.

Patient Cards: Displays name, current keto status (e.g., "In Ketosis", "Needs Review"), and compliance score for the last 24 hours.
Urgent Alerts: Floating notifications for patients who have dropped out of ketosis or missed multiple logs.
Global Search: Quick access to any patient's detailed records.
2. Clinical Analytics & Monitoring
Deep dive into a single patient's metabolic and nutritional data.

Metabolic Trends: Interactive charts showing Blood Ketones (BHB), Glucose, and Weight over time.
Nutrient Breakdown: Comparison of "Actual Intake" vs. "Prescribed Targets" for Carbs, Fat, and Protein.
Compliance Heatmap: A calendar view showing days meeting 100% compliance vs. partial compliance.
3. Prescriptive Diet Management
Tools for doctors to adjust the therapeutic diet protocol.

Macro Target Editor: Dynamic sliders to set daily grams of Fat, Protein, and Carbs.
Keto Ratio Calculator: Automatic calculation of the 3:1 or 4:1 keto ratio based on nutrient inputs.
Meal Plan Selector: Ability to assign pre-vetted meal plans (e.g., "High Fiber Breakfast") from the shared library.
4. Interactive Meal Review
A collaborative space to review parent-logged meals.

Photo Logs: View high-quality images of meals submitted by parents (as seen in the breakfast_meal_plan views).
Approved Food List: A toggle-based interface to mark specific ingredients or meals as "Approved" or "Avoid" for individual patients.
Data Handling Architecture
Patient Medical Records
Demographics: Name, age, gender, patient ID.
Clinical Stats: Baseline weight, target weight, specific keto protocol (Standard, MAD, LGIT).
Prescription Data: Daily targets for Carbs, Fat, Protein, and total Calories.
Longitudinal Monitoring Data
Nutritional Logs: Structured JSON data of every meal (Food Name, Category, calculated Grams).
Compliance Flags: Boolean indicators for whether a meal task was completed.
Metabolic Values: Array of Ketone and Glucose readings with timestamps.
Content & Assets
Meal Library: A central database of meals with images (hosted on LH3 or similar), nutritional categories (e.g., "Healthy Fat"), and base macro values.
Design Aesthetic (The Empathetic Guardian)
The doctor's portal will use the same Vitality Soft design system as the parent app to ensure brand consistency:

Typography: Inter (Bold 700 for headlines, Medium 500 for data labels).
Colors: Primary (#006E2F) for growth, Secondary (#0058BE) for clinical trust.
Atmospheric Depth: No harsh borders; depth achieved via tonal layering (surface-container-low vs surface-container-lowest).
Verification Plan
Interactive Prototype: Validate the "Macro Target Editor" logic ensures the Keto Ratio is accurately calculated.
Data Flow Check: Verify that updates to a patient's prescription on the doctor side immediately reflect in the home_dashboard_with_dated_progress parent view.
Responsive Testing: Ensure the dashboard is usable on both desktop (for in-clinic use) and tablet (for rounds).
