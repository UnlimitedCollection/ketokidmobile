# Design System Strategy: The Empathetic Guardian

This design system is engineered to transform the clinical anxiety of medical monitoring into a warm, editorial experience. We are moving away from "utility-first" aesthetics toward a "high-end wellness" feel. Our goal is to balance the rigor of medical data with the softness of a modern lifestyle brand, ensuring parents feel supported rather than overwhelmed.

---

### 1. Creative North Star: "The Digital Sanctuary"
The Digital Sanctuary rejects the cluttered, line-heavy grids of traditional health apps. Instead, it uses **Atmospheric Depth** and **Intentional Asymmetry**. 

By utilizing generous whitespace (`spacing.16`) and large-scale typography, we create a sense of calm and competence. Layouts should feel organic; allow images to break the container bounds and use "floating" elements to suggest a light, breathable interface. We aren't just building a dashboard; we are curating a care experience.

---

### 2. Colors & Surface Architecture
Our palette uses vibrant, life-affirming tones grounded by a sophisticated neutral base.

- **Primary (`#006E2F`) & Primary Container (`#22C55E`):** Representing vitality and growth. Use the Container variant for large action areas to maintain a "friendly" energy.
- **Secondary (`#0058BE`):** The "Trust" anchor. Reserved for medical data points and professional insights.
- **Tertiary/Accent (`#9D4300`):** Used sparingly for urgent highlights or caloric/keto-specific markers to draw the eye without creating panic.

#### The "No-Line" Rule
**Borders are strictly prohibited for sectioning.** To define boundaries, use tonal shifts. A `surface-container-lowest` card should sit atop a `surface-container-low` background. This creates a natural, soft-touch interface that feels molded rather than assembled.

#### The Glass & Gradient Rule
For high-priority CTAs or "Hero" cards, use a linear gradient transitioning from `primary` to `primary_container` at a 135-degree angle. For floating navigation or modal overlays, apply **Glassmorphism**: use `surface` at 80% opacity with a `20px` backdrop blur to allow the brand colors to bleed through softly.

---

### 3. Typography: Editorial Authority
We use **Inter** not just for legibility, but as a tool for hierarchy.

- **Display & Headline:** Use `headline-lg` (2rem) for daily summaries. These must be **Bold (700)** with tight letter-spacing (-0.02em) to feel like a premium health magazine.
- **Title & Body:** `title-md` should be used for card headers to ensure clear scannability.
- **Micro-Copy:** `label-sm` is reserved for metadata. Even at small scales, keep the contrast high (`on-surface-variant`) to ensure accessibility for tired parents.

*Strategy:* Use extreme scale contrasts. Pair a `display-sm` metric (e.g., "95%") with a `label-md` description to create a sophisticated, data-rich focal point.

---

### 4. Elevation & Depth: Tonal Layering
Traditional drop shadows are too "heavy" for a modern medical app. We achieve lift through the **Layering Principle**.

- **The Stack:** 
  1. Base Layer: `surface` (`#F8F9FB`)
  2. Section Layer: `surface-container-low` (`#F3F4F6`)
  3. Interactive Layer: `surface-container-lowest` (`#FFFFFF`)
- **Ambient Shadows:** Only use shadows on floating elements (like FABs). Use a `40px` blur with 6% opacity, tinted with `primary` (`#006E2F`) rather than pure black. This mimics natural light passing through a translucent object.
- **Ghost Borders:** If a boundary is required for accessibility in forms, use `outline-variant` at 15% opacity. It should be felt, not seen.

---

### 5. Components: The Soft UI Library

#### Cards & Lists
*   **The Forbid Rule:** Never use divider lines.
*   **Implementation:** Separate list items using `spacing.4` (1rem) of vertical whitespace. Group related items on a `surface-container-lowest` card with a `rounded-lg` (2rem) corner radius. This "pill-style" card is the signature of the system.

#### Buttons
*   **Primary:** `primary` background with `on-primary` text. Use `rounded-full` (pill shape) to maximize the "friendly" aesthetic.
*   **Tertiary/Ghost:** No background. Use `primary` text weight 600. These are for low-emphasis actions like "Cancel" or "View History."

#### Input Fields
*   **Style:** Use a "soft-inset" look. Background `surface-container-high`, `rounded-md` (1.5rem), and no border. 
*   **States:** On focus, transition the background to `surface-container-lowest` and add a subtle 2px `primary` ghost-border (20% opacity).

#### Selection Chips
*   For filtering meals or symptoms. Use `surface-container-highest` for unselected and `primary_container` for selected. The transition should feel tactile.

#### Additional App-Specific Components
*   **The Health Gauge:** A semi-circular progress bar using a gradient from `primary_fixed` to `primary`.
*   **Insight Toasts:** Small floating cards using Glassmorphism that "pop" from the bottom to provide real-time encouragement.

---

### 6. Do’s and Don’ts

**Do:**
*   **Do** use `spacing.12` or `spacing.16` for top-level page margins to create a "boutique" feel.
*   **Do** use high-quality, bright food photography that features natural light and soft shadows.
*   **Do** wrap healthcare illustrations in circular `surface-container-low` containers to integrate them into the UI.

**Don’t:**
*   **Don't** use 1px solid black or grey lines. They create visual noise and clinical coldness.
*   **Don't** use "Alert Red" for everything. Use `tertiary` (Orange) for warnings to keep the mood constructive.
*   **Don't** cram data. If a screen feels full, use a horizontal carousel (`spacing.4` gap) instead of a vertical list.

---

### 7. Spacing & Rhythm
Consistency is maintained through a strict 4px-based scale. Use `spacing.8` (2rem) for most internal card padding to ensure the "Modern/Clean" promise of the original brief is met. Large gaps are your friend; they signify confidence and ease of use.
